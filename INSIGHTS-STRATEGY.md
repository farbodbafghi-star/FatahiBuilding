# Fatahi Building — Insights & Content Strategy

## The System

**One longform article per month** on the website → repurposed into **4–6 LinkedIn posts** across three profiles.

This is the entire content engine. Simple, sustainable for a two-person team running active job sites.

---

## Cadence

| What | When | Who |
|------|------|-----|
| Longform article published on website | 1st week of the month | Written from Majid/Farbod input |
| Company LinkedIn post (share article) | Week 1 | Fatahi Building page |
| Majid LinkedIn post (personal angle) | Week 2 | Majid's profile |
| Farbod LinkedIn post (client-facing angle) | Week 2 | Farbod's profile |
| Majid short take (punchy insight) | Week 3 | Majid's profile |

## Content Categories

Rotate through these — never publish two from the same category in a row:

1. **The Build Process** — demystify how custom home building works
2. **Design Decisions** — materials, layouts, the builder-designer perspective
3. **Project Stories** — narratives of completed work (not just photo galleries)
4. **Toronto/GTA Specific** — zoning, permits, climate, neighbourhoods
5. **Investment & Value** — cost realities, renovate vs. rebuild, ROI
6. **Working With Us** — transparency about how Fatahi Building operates

## Article Structure Template

```
TITLE
- Clear, specific, includes a natural keyword
- Under 70 characters ideally

OPENING (2–3 sentences)
- Hook: a real scenario, question, or observation from your work
- Promise: what the reader will learn

CONTEXT (1–2 paragraphs)
- Why this matters for someone building/renovating in Toronto/GTA
- Brief authority signal ("In 30+ years of designing and building...")

BODY (3–5 sections with H2 subheadings)
- One key point per section
- Include at least one real anecdote from your projects
- Toronto/GTA-specific details where relevant
- Scannable subheadings (skimmer should get the gist)

PRACTICAL TAKEAWAY
- Summary, checklist, or "questions to ask"
- Something the reader can actually use

CLOSING (2–3 sentences)
- Subtly reinforce design-build advantage
- Soft CTA: "If you're considering..., we'd welcome a conversation."
```

**Target length:** 1,200–1,800 words. Project stories can be shorter (800–1,000) if photo-heavy.

### Repurposing formula per article:

- **Farbod post:** Practical tips or "what to expect" framing. Second-person, 100–200 words, numbered tips.
- **Company post:** Educational summary + link to full article + project photo.
- **Majid short take:** One punchy insight, under 100 words. Designed for engagement.
- **Farbod BTS:** Photo from a current project + caption tying back to the article's theme.

## Writing Principles

1. **Lead with what you know, not what you sell.** Every article should teach something. The CTA is always soft and at the end.
2. **Use specifics, not generalities.** "We check plumb at three points" > "We pay attention to detail."
3. **Tell the story of the problem.** Challenges you navigated build more trust than "we deliver excellence."
4. **Show the father-son dynamic.** Majid's experience + Farbod's client focus — no competitor can copy this.
5. **Real photos only.** Never stock. Job site photos are gold.
6. **Write for intelligent adults.** No dumbing down, no jargon-heavy showing off. Coffee conversation level.
7. **Every piece answers one of three questions:**
   - "Can I trust these people?"
   - "Do they know what they're doing?"
   - "Is this the right move for me?"
